Private Instruction

Chess Coaching

I work with students of varying ages and skill levels, building uniquely tailored training programs for each. My coaching philosophy is rooted in helping players understand why moves work — not just what to play.

My background spans over a decade of competitive play at the highest scholastic level, coaching at The Dalton School (including as Head Coach for their Summer Chess Camp), and community instruction through programs like DOROT Intergenerational Chess. I've taught everyone from kindergarteners to senior citizens, and from the head of my school to individual national champions. Nothing has been more rewarding than watching a student who first sat down across from me not knowing how the pieces move go on, two years later, to win an individual national scholastic championship — entirely through his own dedication and hard work. I understand what it takes to improve at every level of the game.

Whether you're a complete beginner or a serious competitive player looking to reach a new level, I can help you get there.

Biography

About Me

Gus Huston  ·  Georgetown University

I'm Gus Huston — a FIDE International Master and seasoned competitor in the US Chess scholastic system. I earned my IM title in 2023, capping a career that includes seven individual scholastic national championships, a Pan-American Gold Medal, and a peak FIDE rating of 2451 (top 100 in the U.S. for any age) — and I am currently working towards my Grandmaster title.

I'm currently a sophomore at Georgetown University, where I double major in Russian and Psychology. Outside of my coursework, I work in two research labs, am the president of Georgetown's Chess Club, and volunteer as a Georgetown homelessness outreach coordinator. I also speak five languages, and have a deep interest in the intersection of business, law, and national security.
