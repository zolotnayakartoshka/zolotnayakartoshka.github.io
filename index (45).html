Private Instruction

Chess Coaching

I work with students of varying ages and skill levels, building uniquely tailored training programs for each. My coaching philosophy is rooted in helping players understand why moves work — not just what to play.

My background spans 14 years of competitive play at the highest scholastic level, coaching at The Dalton School, as Head Coach of the Dalton Summer Chess Camp, and in my community through local programs. I've taught everyone from kindergarteners to senior citizens, from the head of my school to individual national champions. Nothing has been more rewarding than watching a student who didn't know how the pieces moved, two years later, go on to win an individual national scholastic championship. Entirely through his own dedication and hard work. I understand what it takes to improve at every level of the game.

Whether you're a complete beginner or a serious competitive player looking to reach a new level, I can help you get there.

Biography

About Me

Gus Huston
Georgetown University '28

I'm Gus Huston, a FIDE International Master and seasoned competitor in the US Chess scholastic system. I earned my IM title in 2023, capping a career that includes seven individual scholastic national championships, a Pan-American Gold Medal, and a peak FIDE rating of 2451 (top 100 in the U.S. for any age) — and I am currently working towards my Grandmaster title.

Beyond the Board

I'm currently a sophomore at Georgetown University, where I double major in Russian and Psychology. Outside of my coursework, I work in two research labs, am the president of Georgetown's Chess Club, and volunteer as a Georgetown homelessness outreach coordinator. When I'm not at the board, I enjoy running, lifting, and boxing. I'm also a nationally registered EMT, a language enthusiast (Russian, Latin, Farsi, Spanish), a recreational coder, and am currently training for my first marathon.
